Examen Civique — CSP Level Study Guide

A study companion for the French civic exam (carte de séjour pluriannuelle level), based on official information from the Ministry of the Interior's civic education site.

What is the Examen Civique?

The examen civique is a new civic knowledge exam created by the French law of January 26, 2024 on immigration and integration. It verifies a candidate's understanding of the principles and values of the Republic, its institutional organization, and how French society functions. Starting January 1, 2026, passing this exam becomes a requirement for anyone applying for their first multi-year residence permit (carte de séjour pluriannuelle, or CSP) or their first ten-year resident card (carte de résident, CR). The CSP level is the standard entry-level tier of the exam.

Who Must Take It

Any foreign national over 18 who has signed the Republican Integration Contract (Contrat d'Intégration Républicaine, CIR) and wishes to settle permanently in France, and who is applying for a first CSP or first CR permit.
  • Some people are exempt: beneficiaries of international protection (refugee status) and nationals covered by certain bilateral agreements.
  • The exam is not required when simply renewing a permit you already hold.
  • A passing CR-level certificate is automatically equivalent to a passing CSP-level certificate.

Exam Format

Format40 multiple-choice questions, in French, 4 options each, 1 correct answer
Question types28 knowledge questions (published bank) + 12 situational questions (not published)
Time limit45 minutes maximum, taken on a tablet or computer
Passing scoreAt least 32/40 correct (80%)
ValidityCertificate never expires; you may retake the exam as many times as needed
Where to registerTwo approved bodies: Chambre de Commerce et d'Industrie de Paris (CCIP) and France Éducation International (FEI)

The Five Official Themes

1. Principles and values of the Republic

National motto and symbols (the tricolor flag, Marianne, the rooster, La Marseillaise), the meaning of "Liberté, Égalité, Fraternité," and laïcité (secularism) — the separation of religion and state that guarantees freedom of belief and institutional neutrality, notably at school.

2. Institutional and political system

How the executive (President + government), legislative (National Assembly + Senate), and judicial branches work and interact; election rules and terms of office; local administration (préfet, mayor, départements); and France's role in the European Union.

Key numbers: President's term = 5 years · Deputies (Assemblée nationale) = 577 seats, 5-year term · Senators = 6-year term · Voting age = 18 · EU member states (as of Jan 1, 2025) = 27 · French départements ≈ 101

3. Rights and duties

Foundational texts (the 1789 Declaration of the Rights of Man and Citizen), individual freedoms and their legal limits, the justice system (police vs. gendarmerie, types of offenses), and civic duties such as respecting the law, helping others in danger, and reducing waste.

4. History, geography and culture

Major historical milestones from the 1789 Revolution through the Fifth Republic (founded 1958 by Charles de Gaulle); key wars and dates (WWI 1914–1918, WWII 1939–1945); abolition of slavery (1848); free public schooling (since 1881, thanks to Jules Ferry); basic geography (capital, rivers, seas, regions, overseas territories); and notable cultural figures (Molière, Camus, Simone de Beauvoir, Josephine Baker, and others).

5. Living in French society

Practical daily-life knowledge: emergency numbers (15 SAMU, 18 fire department, 112 general), the healthcare system (Sécurité sociale, médecin traitant, Carte Vitale), employment rules (SMIC minimum wage, 35-hour work week, France Travail), family/civil procedures (civil marriage, birth registration within 5 days), and compulsory schooling (ages 3–16, culminating in the Baccalauréat).

How to Prepare

  • Preparation is free — the ministry explicitly warns that no legitimate resource should charge for practice questions, and that paid sites are not government-affiliated and carry a fraud risk.
  • Use the official fact sheets and the published CSP knowledge-question list on formation-civique.interieur.gouv.fr.
  • Practice with the free 50-question quiz built by the association France Fraternité (note the question format there may differ slightly from the real exam).
  • Review the accompanying bilingual flashcards (190 cards covering all five themes) alongside this guide.
